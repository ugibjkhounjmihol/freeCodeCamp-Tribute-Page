Dr. Norman Borlaug
Dr. Norman Borlaug, third from the left, trains biologists in Mexico on how to increase wheat yields - part of his life-long war on hunger.
Here is a timeline of Dr. Borlaug's life:
  • 1914: Born in Cresco, Iowa, USA.
  • 1942: Earned a Ph.D. in plant pathology and genetics from the University of Minnesota.
  • 1944: Moved to Mexico to work on wheat production, leading to the development of high-yield, disease-resistant wheat varieties.
  • 1963: His work helped launch the Green Revolution, transforming agricultural practices globally.
  • 1970: Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his contributions to fighting world hunger.
  • 1984: Founded the World Food Prize to honor individuals who advanced human development through agriculture.
  • 2009: Passed away at the age of 95, leaving behind a legacy of combating hunger and improving global food security.
